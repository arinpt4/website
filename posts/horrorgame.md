one generational horror game i ALWAYS come back to every year is Fran Bow by killmonday games. 

the artstyle, atmosphere, and story are so good and i cannot recommend it enough if you are looking for a good point and click horror game.

i was thinking about it today and realized what an oxymoron a cozy horror game is. horror games are supposed to scare you and usually are supposed to be one and done. 

a huge reason that it's "cozy" for me is because i watched markiplier play it when i was a kid.

it scared me so bad, but i kept watching regardless. 

every couple of years i would watch someone else's playthrough of it and eventually once i discovered game theory, all the theory videos i could find.

i think overall though, fran bow's phenominal artstyle really made it stick with me. some scenes were genuinely haunting and scared me so bad as a kid.

but this year, i decided i would man up and play it instead of watching a let's play like i always do.

and it was amazing. it's a completely different experience and i highly recommend playing it yourself. even though i basically knew everything that would happen, i still got jumpscared in a couple places. but actually playing the game also meant that i got to look at the art closer and it is haunting to say the least. 

anyways, another one that's really worth the watch is petscop. yes, i know its technically not a game but it has such an amazing artstyle and story. i can't get into it too much without spoiling but if you really enjoy watching theories i linked a theory i think solved the whole game.

i guess for me something that makes a cozy horror game is a horror game i watched as a kid that scared the hell outta me.
